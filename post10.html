So, just to summarize ...

Lots of things learned this quarter

Posted by Colleen Cushnie on June 17, 2019

Is this really the end?

The red end or the blue end?

Over the last 10 weeks, I've learned all sorts of interesting things about data analytics and Google Analytics in particular, and I've shared my thoughts and experiences with you. And now here we are at the end of the quarter and now I'm here to summarize what I've discovered.

Goals and Geography

You've read all the posts, prepare for a summary

One of the goals I had for this class was to be drive traffic from my personal website to this website and vice versa. I had friends look at the site via mobile. I had classmates check it out via Slack. And then of course I used Facebook to promomte the blog. This drove some great numbers for initial clicks. Not to mention I got some good hits from outside of the US.

Still haven't found out who's in the Philippines.

However for my personal site, I've generated some amazing numbers and a few places I didn't expect.

I wonder who's in Denmark

What's also fun is to drill down into the details of where people are. Looks like I have a good showing in Seattle and Atlanta.

That 4% is interesting

Big should out to Mom & Dad for being my two users in Smyrna. And for the record, they're the one sponsoring me in this program. So thanks you guys! I bet you had no idea that you'd have your information blasted around the world.

so much info

Looks really do matter

The test that stood out to me was the A/B testing that I conducted. When I took a moment to look at my personal website for some edits and updates, what I noticed is that the link to this blog was very subtle. But that's the whole point of my website - it's a grayscale color scheme and things are meant to be jumping out at you making your eyes water. And as pleasing as it is, it defeated the purpose of driving traffic to this blog.

Maybe too subtle.

So I changed it to a button and I had an immediate uptick in visitors.

This works.

Colleen! You've just completed your final blog entry. What are you gonna do now?

I'm taking from this class a new understanding of how Google Analytics work. Although I knew that my data was being tracked and as much as I turn off my cookies, I'm still leaving a little trail of cookie crumbs and digital prints everywhere.

It really is.

What I also learned is that small tweaks can make huge differences and that there are going to be many a strategy meeting in my future where someone will pull out the charts and show us the bounce rates, the goals, and the funnels we'll need to create to get those numbers where they want. Because after all time and data is money.

Now it's time for me to say thanks for following along. Keep your eyes peeled for a different blog and even more random memes.
