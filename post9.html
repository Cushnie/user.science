Hello Captain Not-So-Obvious

This experiment is a continuation of my goals project in hopes of driving traffic from my personal website to this one. If you don't remember what it was, feel free to read about it here.

Short and Sweet

Stan says, "No."

So, I found myself spending more time on my final WATS4000 project than trying to track my data. And unfortunately that was also coming from the mindset of "Aahhh ... analytics & data is another department's problem and I'll worry about it when I become a freelancing consultant on my boat in Tahiti." But this has backfired for me in my scholastics yet my final project was done *almost* ahead of schedule.

Talk about win some & lose some.

The world is one big A/B test. -some guy on the internet

Pay attention to me!!! Click here!

Ne'er truer word spoken.

One thing I also realized is that my primary website is very subtle but I guess I don't have enough on there for the user to be enticed to look in the upper left corner. Grayscale is aesthetically pleasing but if I'm going to drum up traffic and direct them to where I want to go, I'm going to have to be a little more obvious.

Though, after digging deeper and looking into the data, it looks like I did manage to create some traffic to the site. So I guess it wasn't a total failure.

is that a 5000% increase???? w00t!

Final thoughts

I went into this experiment knowing that I had a challenge to get people to do what I wanted. And given my lack of Jedi mind tricks and Vulcan mind meld skills, I was actually surprised at that a small subtle tweak of a color is exactly what I needed. So I've kept the change and hopefully that will continue to yield the results I need.
