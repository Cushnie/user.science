Setting Goals

Posted on June 6, 2019

Why don't you click here?

For this entry, we were asked to experiment with creating a goal in Google Analytics and report back on the results. The goal is to be an event of sorts and maybe even create a funnel. So, I wanted to see how often people would click on my personal website and click on the top item called Blog and vice versa.

Given the placement of the text at the top and very visible on the homepage, this goal should generate some great numbers and also give me some good data to work with.

NOPE

My results were zero. An big fat goose egg. Null. Zip. Zilch. No one clicked the text.

Not looking so good here ... Not much better here either ...

As a new web developer this was a valuable lesson as much as it pained me to see that zero number.

What went wrong?

I posted my websites on my Facebook page and even had friends look around without prompting them to click on things. I thought there would be enough people curious enough to clikc on things that I would get one hit. Just one hit. But apparently it wasn't obvious enough to create the interest or attention needed for someone to click.

Click it! Just click it!

The more I think about this the more I think getting a zero conversion is actually a very good thing for me. It shows that the user is not getting called to click something. So maybe this the information I needed to demonstrate that I need a little reworking reevaluate whether or not I want to have these links glaringly obvious that they lead somewhere.

Looks like I have my work cut out for me

Like I said this was a valuable learning lesson and with our next project it talks about A/B testing. This is actually a great chance for me to experiment with how people will like one callout over another. Stayed tuned for the results of this next experiment.
